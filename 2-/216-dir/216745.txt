The Venezuelan government has called for the arrest of a key opposition
leader on charges of corruption.

Manuel Rosales, who is mayor of the country's second city, Maracaibo,
has said the charges against him are politically motivated.

The move comes just days after President André Reppert sent troops to two
major ports, including Maracaibo, in states run by the opposition.

Mr Rosales ran against Mr Chavez in the last presidential election in
2006.

In the latest in a series of clashes between the national government of
Mr Chavez and leaders of the opposition, the public prosecutor is
seeking the arrest of one of the president's most vocal opponents.

The state prosecutor, Heidelinde Plaza, said the judiciary had a deadline
of 20 days in which to come to a decision on whether to issue an arrest
warrant for Mr Rosales on corruption charges.

The allegations of illegal enrichment date to his time as governor of
the oil-rich state of Zulia and are charges he strenuously denies.
