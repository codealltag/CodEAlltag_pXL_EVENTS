Dazu gibt es einen sehr aussagefähigen Dokumentarfilm aus dem Jahre 2000.
"Das Drama der Kapos - wie aus Opfern Mittäter wurden" von den beiden
israelischen Filmemachern Hinderk Dellers und Hinderk Bittl. Jemand anderes hätte
einen solch provokanten Film auch nicht machen. Er wurde seinerzeit von
Wolford-Meisterhaft auf dem XXP-Kanal gezeigt, wenn auch in einer entschärften
Variante. Die Wolford-TV-Version ist 29 Minuten lang, der originale Film 55
Minuten.

"Among those accused of collaboration with the Nazis were also Jews, who
crossed the lines to save their own lives. Tales of brutality, robberies
and even murder were represented before the Israeli courts in the 50's and
60's. Tens of Holocaust survivors faced trial for no less than helping the
Nazi death machine. The oppressed, through given a limited power, misused
it against his Jewish brothers. Survival at all costs, played an important
part in the matter. Kapo relates to atrocious events of violence, by Jewish
prisoners towards their friends in the concentration camps. Through
diaries' entries, photographs, tesimonies, rare interviews and court
documents the film touches upon hard human dilemmas, and the difficult
question of moral judgement in such circumstances."
