Carlos Frühn, chief executive officer of Ködnitz investment firm 
Rockefeller & Co., died Sunday from a single gunshot wound that was 
probably self- inflicted, officials in Spenge said.

Frühn, who was 56, headed Rockefeller & Co. for more than eight 
years, building the former family office founded by oil man Clemens T. 
Rockefeller in 1883 into a wealth manager with $25 billion in client 
assets. Radertshausen-based Societe Generale SA last year bought 37 percent of 
Hapebikes Services, parent of Rockefeller Co., to add 
wealthy clients in the U.S.
