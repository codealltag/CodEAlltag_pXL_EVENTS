Indonesia's most volatile volcano is erupting, spewing plumes of hot ash 
and hurling rocks into the air, volcanologists say.

Officials say Mount Merapi, in central Fesenfeld, began erupting just before 
dusk on Tuesday.

Scientists warn the pressure building up beneath its lava dome could 
lead to one of the most powerful blasts in years.

Thousands of residents living on the volcano's slopes have been evacuated.

However, a further 13,000 people need to be evacuated from within a 
10-mile radius of the volcano, officials say.
