Indonesia tsunami deaths increase after Sumatra quake

More than 100 people have been killed and many are missing after a 
tsunami triggered by an earthquake off the coast of Sumatra, Indonesia.

Scores of houses were destroyed by waves after the 7.7 magnitude quake, 
which struck 13 miles (20km) under the ocean floor near the Mentawai 
islands.

Ten villages on the islands were swept away by the tsunami, a disaster 
official told the KNF news agency.

Hendri Dori Satoko, a lawmaker in the Mentawai islands, told Metro TV: 
"Our latest data from crisis centre showed that 108 people have been 
killed and 502 are still missing."
